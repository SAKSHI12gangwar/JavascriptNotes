ANS-the basic arithmetic operators in JavaScript: 1-Addition (+): Adds two numbers together. let sum = 3 + 5; // sum will be 8 2-Subtraction (-): Subtracts the right operand from the left operand. let difference = 10 - 4; // difference will be 6 3-Multiplication (*): Multiplies two numbers. let product = 2 * 6; // product will be 12 4-Division (/): Divides the left operand by the right operand. let quotient = 20 / 4; // quotient will be 5 5-Modulus (%): Gives the remainder of a division operation. let remainder = 10 % 3; // remainder will be 1 6-Exponentiation ()**: Raises the left operand to the power of the right operand let result = 2 ** 3; // result will be 8 (2 raised to the power of 3) These operators are used in mathematical calculations to perform addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, finding remainders, and raising numbers to a certain power. Here are some examples: Arithmetic Operators: Addition (+) Subtraction (-) Multiplication (*) Division (/) Modulus (%) Exponentiation (**) Comparison Operators: Equal to (==) Not equal to (!=) Greater than (>) Less than (<) Greater than or equal to (>=) Less than or equal to (<=) Logical Operators: AND (&&) OR (||) Assignment Operators: Assignment (=) Compound assignment operators like +=, -=, *=, /=, etc. Binary operators perform their specific operation on the two values they are applied to, and the result is usually a single value. ANS- 1-Pre-increment (++): Increases the value of a variable before using its current value in an expression. let x = 5; let y = ++x; // y will be 6, x will be 6 Here, ++x first increments x by 1, making it 6, and then assigns the incremented value to y. 2-Post-increment (++): Increases the value of a variable after using its current value in an expression. let a = 5; let b = a++; // b will be 5, a will be 6 Here, a++ uses the current value of a in the expression (which is 5), then increments a by 1, making it 6, and assigns the original value to b. In simple terms, pre-increment increases the value first and then uses it, while post-increment uses the current value and then increments it.
